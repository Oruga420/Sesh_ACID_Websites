# Design Directives — Randy Selzer, Sutton Group Summit Realty

## Identity Summary
Randy Selzer is a seasoned Platinum award-winning real estate agent with 30+ years of experience in Mississauga, Oakville, and the GTA. His brand exudes trusted authority, warmth, and deep local expertise. He's not a flashy startup agent — he's a community fixture with a real estate podcast and thousands of successful transactions. The redesign should feel like a premium, established professional firm — not a tech-forward disruptor.

## DO
- Use warm whites and clean layouts to convey professionalism and trust
- Feature Randy's professional headshot prominently in the hero or about section
- Highlight 30+ years of experience and Platinum Award status above the fold
- Feature the podcast prominently — it's a unique differentiator (73 episodes, 33,900+ downloads)
- Use Mississauga skyline imagery to reinforce local expertise
- Use gold/amber accents for award badges and premium callouts — mirrors his gold tie
- Keep navigation minimal: Home, About, Services, Listings, Contact (5 items max)
- Use testimonials with real attribution early in the page flow
- Show CREA, OREA, TRREB memberships as trust badges
- Use split-hero layout: strong headline left, headshot or cityscape right
- Make phone number and email tappable and prominent

## DON'T
- Don't use dark mode — Randy's existing sites are all light-themed
- Don't use playful or casual fonts — this is a 30-year professional practice
- Don't use Playfair Display — use Cormorant Garamond for elegant authority
- Don't use stock images of generic houses — use Mississauga skyline photos
- Don't add a stats bar with fabricated metrics
- Don't make this look like a tech startup landing page
- Don't use rounded pill buttons — use subtle 6px radius for professionalism
- Don't use a dark hero overlay that hides the imagery
- Don't use Inter or Roboto for body text — Lato is already his brand font

## Font Pairing
Heading: Cormorant Garamond — an elegant serif that communicates decades of heritage and authority, distinguished from the common Playfair Display
Body: Lato — already used on Randy's Google Sites, maintaining brand continuity. Clean, friendly, highly readable.
Nav: Lato at 500 weight, uppercase with tracking — professional and organized

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: #1B5E7A (teal) on white cards — professional, trustworthy blue
- Gold accent: #C8963E for award badges, pricing highlights, premium callouts
- Section alternation: #FAFAF7 (warm white) → #F0EDE6 (sand) → #FFFFFF (card white) → #1A2332 (dark footer)
- Never use bright red or orange — clashes with the professional authority tone
- Hero text: white on dark overlay over Mississauga skyline
- Cards: white with subtle border #DDD8CE and soft shadow

## Layout Archetype: SERVICE-FIRST
Rationale: Randy is a service-driven real estate professional. His authority comes from credentials, testimonials, and market expertise — not a visual portfolio. Trust drives conversions, not aesthetics alone.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. Hero — split layout with strong headline and Mississauga skyline / headshot
2. Services — clear service offerings for buyers, sellers, and investors
3. About — Randy's story, 30+ years, Saskatchewan origin, community roots
4. Testimonials — real client praise emphasizing trust and results
5. Podcast — featured section with episode player and podcast links
6. Contact — map, office address, phone, email, booking CTA

## Layout Blueprint
- Header: Sticky white, logo/name left, 5 nav items right (uppercase Lato), "Contact" CTA button
- Hero: Split — left side has overline "PLATINUM AWARD-WINNING REALTOR" + H1 headline + CTA buttons. Right side has professional headshot or Mississauga skyline
- Services: Three columns — Buyers, Sellers, Investors — with clear descriptions
- About: Two-column — headshot left, story right with credentials
- Testimonials: Cards with client quotes, names, olive checkmark icons
- Podcast: Featured section with cover art, episode highlights, platform links
- Contact: Map + contact form side by side, address and phone prominent
- Footer: Dark (#1A2332), 4 columns — contact info, quick links, social icons, brokerage disclaimer

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "30 Years. Thousands of Happy Clients. Your Next Move."
- Body copy should sound like: "Randy brings three decades of market expertise to every transaction, ensuring you get the best possible outcome — whether you're buying your first home or selling an investment property."
- CTAs should sound like: "Let's Talk About Your Home" / "Start Your Search" / "Get a Free Market Analysis"
