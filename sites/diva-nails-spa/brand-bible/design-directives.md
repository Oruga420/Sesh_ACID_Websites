# Design Directives — Diva Nails & Head Spa

## Layout Personality: GALLERY-FORWARD + NEIGHBORHOOD (B+D Hybrid)

### The One Thing
The hero should immediately communicate "this is a nail salon AND head spa that feels premium but accessible." The split hero with nail art imagery on one side and a warm, inviting headline on the other sets the tone. The head spa is the differentiator — feature it prominently.

### Font Pairing Rationale
- **Cormorant Garamond** (headings): High stroke contrast serif that feels luxurious and salon-appropriate. The elegance matches the pampering brand voice without being generic.
- **Nunito Sans** (body): Warm, rounded sans-serif that feels approachable. The softness of the letterforms matches the "caring" and "welcoming" brand personality.
- Together they create a "luxury you can afford" feel — premium enough for a spa, warm enough for a neighborhood salon.

### Color Usage Rules
- **Mauve pink (#D4A0B9)** is the signature accent — use for buttons, active nav states, highlighted prices, and the booking CTA. Never overuse it; it should feel like a touch of polish.
- **Warm taupe (#8B6F5E)** grounds the design — use for footer text, secondary labels, icon fills on light backgrounds.
- **Gold-blush (#E8C4A0)** is for subtle accents — badge backgrounds, section dividers, price highlights.
- **Page background is warm cream (#FDF8F5)** — NOT stark white. The warmth is essential to the brand feel.
- **Dark sections use espresso brown (#3D2E2A)** — the footer and one contrast section (testimonials or CTA). Never pure black.

### Layout Blueprint

#### Homepage (`/`)
1. **Hero**: Split layout — left: headline + subtitle + "Book Now" CTA. Right: hero image (nail art close-up). Warm cream background with subtle gradient overlay.
2. **Services Teaser**: 3-4 service category cards (Nails, Head Spa, Lashes, Waxing) with icons or small images. Links to `/services`.
3. **Gallery Preview**: 3-4 nail art photos in asymmetric grid. Link to `/gallery`.
4. **CTA Strip**: Full-width mauve pink background — "Ready to Relax?" with booking button.
5. That's it. Keep it tight. Make them click to explore.

#### Services (`/services`)
Full service listing with categories, prices, and durations. Editorial style — NOT card grid. Categories: Head Spa (featured first), Nails, Gel/Acrylic, Shellac, Waxing, Lashes, Facials.

#### Gallery (`/gallery`)
Masonry or asymmetric grid of nail art photos. Dark background section to make images pop. Use all 4 available images prominently.

#### Team (`/team`)
Staff profiles with initials (no photos available) on brand-colored backgrounds. Names, roles, and brief bios.

#### Contact (`/contact`)
Map (prominent), hours, phone, address, booking CTA. The map should be a featured element, not a small embed.

## DO
- Use warm cream backgrounds, not stark white
- Feature head spa as THE differentiator (it's the viral service)
- Use asymmetric image layouts in gallery sections
- Show real prices — they're accessible and competitive
- Use "Scarborough" in copy — local pride matters
- Keep nav labels clear: Home, Services, Gallery, Team, Contact
- Use booking CTA above the fold
- Style the text logo "DIVA NAILS & HEAD SPA" in Cormorant Garamond with generous letter-spacing
- Make the head spa 60/90 min options prominent

## DON'T
- Don't use dark mode — this is a warm, light-themed brand
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter
- Don't add a stats bar ("X+ years, Y+ clients") — fabricated filler
- Don't use stock photos or Unsplash images
- Don't make it feel clinical/medical — it's a PAMPERING experience
- Don't use anchor navigation — every section gets its own page route
- Don't use 4 equal service cards — use editorial list style for services
- Don't make the hero generic ("Welcome to Diva Nails") — lead with value/emotion
- Don't use pure black (#000) anywhere — always warm dark (#2D2226 or #3D2E2A)
- Don't put all content on the homepage — keep it tight, make them click

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Warm but professional: "Your relaxation starts here" not "Come get your nails did"
- Pampering language: "treat yourself", "expert care", "your moment"
- Local: "Scarborough's destination for..." not "We serve clients in the GTA"
- Inclusive: They serve everyone — men's services are listed too
- Vietnamese-owned: Subtle nod acceptable but don't tokenize
