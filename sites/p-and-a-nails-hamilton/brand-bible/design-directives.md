# Design Directives — P & A Nails Hamilton Mountain

## Identity Summary
P & A Nails is a family-run nail salon on Hamilton Mountain that has built deep loyalty over 10+ years through warmth, patience, and consistently excellent nail work. Janet and her husband run the salon with a small team (Paul, Sally) who are praised by name in reviews. The brand is unpretentious, welcoming, and neighbourhood-rooted — not luxury, not trendy. The site should feel like walking into the salon: warm, comfortable, and familiar.

## DO
- Use warm coral (#ff887d) as the primary accent — it's their actual brand color
- Keep backgrounds light and clean (white + warm linen #e9e0db)
- Use Droid Serif throughout — it's their actual site font, gives warmth without being fussy
- Show nail art gallery prominently — 1800+ IG posts prove this is their pride
- Feature real testimonials with customer names — loyalty stories (15 years, 10 years) are powerful
- Include booking CTA above the fold — this is a walk-in/appointment business
- Show hours and address prominently — neighbourhood foot traffic matters
- Use real salon interior photos — they have good storefront and interior shots
- Use warm, tactile background textures (soft linen, cream sections)
- Keep pricing visible and organized — transparency is on-brand
- Reference the Mountain community — "Hamilton Mountain's neighbourhood nail salon"

## DON'T
- Don't use dark backgrounds for main sections — this is a light, warm, welcoming brand
- Don't use luxury/boutique language — they're affordable and approachable
- Don't add stats bars — no fabricated metrics
- Don't use stock photos — use their real gallery and interior images
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter — Droid Serif is the actual brand font
- Don't make it look clinical or corporate — it's a family salon, not a med-spa
- Don't hide pricing behind CTAs — they display prices openly
- Don't use sharp, angular, modern-minimal design — the brand is warm and rounded
- Don't add "As Seen In" or press sections — not applicable
- Don't create a dark moody design — the salon is bright and welcoming

## Font Pairing
- **Heading**: Droid Serif Bold (700) — warm, readable, classic serif with personality
- **Body**: Droid Serif Regular (400) — matches their actual typography, cohesive feel
- **Nav**: Droid Serif Regular (400) with 0.03em letter-spacing
- **Rationale**: Single-font family maintains the unpretentious, consistent character. Droid Serif is warm without being stuffy, classic without being old-fashioned. Contrast comes from weight (400 vs 700) and scale, not font switching.

## Color Usage Rules
- **Coral Blush (#ff887d)**: Primary buttons, CTA backgrounds, accent borders, active nav states, pricing highlights
- **Warm Linen (#e9e0db)**: Alternating section backgrounds, testimonial cards, about section
- **Soft Rose (#fdb1aa)**: Hover states, subtle backgrounds, decorative accents
- **White (#ffffff)**: Primary backgrounds, card backgrounds, text on dark sections
- **Near-Black (#222222)**: Headings, footer background
- **Dark Gray (#444444)**: Body text
- **Medium Gray (#666666)**: Captions, secondary text, meta info

## Layout Archetype: GALLERY-FORWARD + NEIGHBORHOOD
A hybrid that leads with visual work samples (nail art portfolio) while grounding in neighbourhood identity (Hamilton Mountain, hours, location).

## Section Order
1. **Hero**: Storefront image background with warm overlay, headline + booking CTA
2. **Gallery Teaser**: 4-6 nail art images in asymmetric grid, link to full gallery page
3. **Services Teaser**: Top 3-4 service categories with starting prices, link to full services page
4. **Testimonials Teaser**: 2-3 powerful loyalty quotes
5. **CTA**: Full-width coral section with booking prompt

## Layout Blueprint
- **Homepage**: Tight — hero + gallery peek + services peek + testimonials peek + CTA. NOT a section dump.
- **Gallery page**: Full nail art grid with real images from their website gallery
- **Services page**: Organized by category (Manicure, Pedicure, Nails, Waxing, Packages) with clear pricing
- **About page**: Story, team (Janet, Paul, Sally with initials), salon photos, features
- **Contact page**: Map, hours, phone, address, contact form, booking CTA

## Tone of Voice Guide
- First-person plural ("We" / "Our team")
- Warm, conversational, not salesy
- Short sentences, friendly tone
- Reference the community: "Hamilton Mountain", "the neighbourhood", "our regulars"
- Emphasize: warmth, care, skill, affordability, loyalty
- Never: pretentious, clinical, corporate, trendy jargon
