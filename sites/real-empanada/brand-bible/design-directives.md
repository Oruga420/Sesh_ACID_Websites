# Design Directives -- Real Empanada

## Identity Summary
Real Empanada is a warm, family-run Colombian empanada business rooted in heritage and authenticity. The visual identity centres on a cream/warm-earth palette with gold and green accents, friendly rounded typography (Averia Sans Libre), and a focus on real food photography. The brand feels like a neighbourhood kitchen -- approachable, proud of its Colombian roots, and unpretentious.

## DO
- Use warm cream (#fffceb) as the primary page background -- matches their actual site
- Use dark navy (#253544) for the footer and dark sections
- Lead with real food photography -- empanadas, chicharron, salsa jars, paletas
- Keep headings in Averia Sans Libre -- their actual heading font, friendly and distinctive
- Use Nunito Sans for body text (closest Google Fonts match to their Brandon Grotesque)
- Emphasize "Mama's Signature Salsa" and "100% Authentic Colombian" messaging
- Show multiple ordering options (UberEats, DoorDash, curbside, delivery, wholesale)
- Include green (#31a16e) for CTAs and fresh/natural accents
- Use gold/amber (#dd9933) for premium/heritage accents
- Show the Beaches storefront and food close-ups prominently
- Include gluten-free and halal badges prominently

## DON'T
- Don't use Playfair Display or Inter -- these are NOT their fonts
- Don't use dark backgrounds for the main page -- their site is cream/warm
- Don't use cold/corporate blue tones -- the brand is warm and earthy
- Don't use stock photos -- Real Empanada has plenty of real food photography
- Don't use overly formal or corporate language -- the tone is warm and casual
- Don't make the design feel "fine dining" -- it's a neighbourhood empanada shop
- Don't bury the ordering CTAs -- they're a conversion-driven food business

## Font Pairing
Heading: Averia Sans Libre -- their actual website heading font, friendly handwritten feel
Body: Nunito Sans -- closest Google Fonts match to Brandon Grotesque (their body font via Adobe Typekit)
Nav: Averia Sans Libre at 700 weight -- consistent with their site navigation

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: #31a16e (green) on cream/white background
- Section alternation: #fffceb (cream) -> #ffffff (white) -> #fffceb (cream)
- Footer: #253544 (dark navy) with white text
- Accent highlights: #dd9933 (gold) and #ee9982 (coral) for warmth
- Never use pure black backgrounds -- the brand is warm, not moody
- Never use cold blue as a primary -- reserve #35485d only for body text

## Layout Archetype: LOCATION-FIRST
Rationale: Real Empanada is a restaurant/manufacturer with multiple locations, farmers market roots, and strong neighbourhood identity. Hours, address, and ordering methods drive conversions.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. Hero -- full-bleed empanada photo with "100% Authentic Colombian Empanadas" headline and ORDER NOW CTA
2. Menu/Featured Items -- empanadas, salsa, paletas, tacos with real photos and prices
3. Story -- Richard's journey from Motivos to farmer's markets to brick-and-mortar
4. Gallery -- food close-ups, storefront, preparation shots
5. Reviews -- verbatim customer quotes (4.7 star average)
6. Locations -- Beaches address, hours, map, ordering options
7. Contact -- phone, email, social links

## Layout Blueprint
- Header: Sticky, cream background, logo left, nav right, green CTA button
- Hero: Full-bleed food photo with dark overlay (rgba 0,0,0,0.5), centered white text, large green ORDER NOW button
- Menu section: Grid of cards with real food photos, product names, and prices on cream background
- Story section: Split layout with Richard's photo and origin narrative
- Gallery: Masonry or grid of food and storefront photos
- Reviews: Customer quotes in cards with star ratings
- Locations: Map embed, hours, address, delivery partners
- Footer: Dark navy (#253544), 3 columns, social links, contact info

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "100% Authentic Colombian Empanadas" or "Toronto's Favourite"
- Body copy should sound like: "Delicious, wholesome, authentic Colombian empanadas made with fresh, locally sourced ingredients"
- CTAs should sound like: "Order Now" or "Get Directions" -- direct and friendly
