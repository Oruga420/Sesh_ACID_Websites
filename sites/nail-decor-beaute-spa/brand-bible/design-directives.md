# Design Directives — Nail Decor Beaute & Spa

## Brand Personality Answers

1. **Dominant emotion**: Warmth and relaxation — a pampering escape
2. **Product type**: The WORK is the product — nail art, lash extensions, spa treatments
3. **Establishment**: Newer salon building reputation — clean/modern aesthetic
4. **Customer feeling**: Relaxed, pampered, welcomed — bright and clean atmosphere
5. **What would NEVER fit**: Dark/grungy aesthetic, sharp industrial angles, masculine typography, aggressive CTAs

## Layout Personality: GALLERY-FORWARD + NEIGHBORHOOD (B+D Hybrid)

The nail art IS the product. Gallery images need prominence. But as a neighborhood salon in Scarborough, hours/address/booking must be immediately accessible.

## DO

- Use warm blush/rose tones as the dominant color story
- Feature nail art prominently — gallery is key
- Show services with clear pricing — transparency builds trust
- Keep the booking CTA visible at all times (sticky or repeated)
- Use soft rounded corners (16px) on cards and images
- Use elegant serif headings (Cormorant Garamond) for sophistication
- Vary section padding: hero breathes (py-32), services compact (py-16), gallery tight
- Include a full-bleed dark section for testimonials (contrast inversion)
- Use one asymmetric gallery layout — large feature + smaller grid
- Address and hours visible within first two scrolls
- Gold accents for premium service tiers

## DON'T

- Don't use dark mode — this is a light, bright, feminine space
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter — they're generic defaults
- Don't add a stats bar — no fabricated "1000+ happy clients" claims
- Don't use stock photos from Unsplash — CSS gradients with brand colors for fallback
- Don't use equal 4-card grids — vary the layout rhythm
- Don't use anchor navigation — every nav item gets its own page
- Don't make the homepage a dump of all sections — hero + teaser + CTA only
- Don't use heavy drop shadows — keep it soft and feminine
- Don't use bold/aggressive typography weights on body text
- Don't skip the booking CTA — it should appear minimum 3 times per page

## Font Pairing Rationale

- **Cormorant Garamond** (headings): Elegant, feminine serif with beautiful contrast between thick/thin strokes. Perfect for a beauty/spa brand. Feels sophisticated without being stuffy.
- **Nunito Sans** (body): Rounded, friendly sans-serif that pairs well with the elegance of Cormorant. Easy to read, approachable, warm.

## Color Usage Rules

- Primary rose (#D4A0A0): CTAs, accents, active states, decorative elements
- Dark brown (#3D2B2B): Footer, testimonial section bg, high-contrast text areas
- Off-white (#FFF9F7): Main background — warm, not clinical white
- Blush tint (#FDF0ED): Alternating section backgrounds
- Gold (#C9A96E): Premium service indicators, special badges

## Layout Blueprint

### Homepage
- Full-bleed hero with warm overlay, centered headline, booking CTA
- 3-4 gallery images as teaser linking to /gallery
- Quick services overview (3 featured) linking to /services
- Single testimonial highlight

### Services Page
- Editorial list layout with dividers, not card grid
- Categories: Nails, Pedicures, Waxing, Lash Extensions
- Each with clear pricing
- Booking CTA after each category

### Gallery Page
- Edge-to-edge masonry layout
- Dark background to make nail art pop
- Asymmetric grid: 1 large + multiple small

### Contact Page
- Map as featured element (half-page)
- Hours prominently displayed
- Booking CTA large and centered
- All contact methods: phone, email, Instagram

## Tone of Voice Guide

- Warm and inviting, like a friend recommending their favorite spot
- Professional but not corporate
- Use "you" language — "Your nails deserve this"
- Emphasize the experience, not just the service
- Subtly aspirational — "treat yourself" energy
