# Design Directives — Courage My Love

## Identity Summary
Courage My Love is a 50-year Kensington Market institution — a vibrant, eclectic vintage shop with a signature blue storefront. The brand feels like stepping into a global bazaar curated by a family of passionate treasure hunters. The visual identity should evoke warmth, discovery, and heritage — colorful and layered but never chaotic. Think: a well-organized treasure chest, not a cluttered thrift store.

## DO
- Use the signature Courage My Love blue (#2A7CB8) as the primary brand color — it IS the brand
- Keep backgrounds warm and creamy (#F5F0E6, #E8DCC8) — this is a warm, inviting space
- Use full-bleed product/gallery images — the treasures sell themselves
- Show the blue storefront prominently — it's iconic and instantly recognizable
- Use earthy, warm typography colors (#2D1F14 espresso, #4A3728 bark)
- Include the Scriver family story — 50 years, three generations, Stewart still crafting at 86
- Emphasize global sourcing — Mexico, Indonesia, Egypt, Afghanistan
- Show real product photos: beads, vintage clothing, teacups, jewelry, scarves
- Use editorial-style sections with large typography for the heritage story
- Include hours and address prominently — this is a foot-traffic business
- Feature the $5 rack and treasure-hunt experience in messaging
- Use warm amber/gold (#D4A024) for accents and highlights — matches the antique/jewelry aesthetic

## DON'T
- Don't use a dark theme — the store is colorful, bright, warm
- Don't use rounded/pill buttons — this is a heritage brand, keep corners slightly sharp (4px)
- Don't use playful/comic fonts — Hammersmith One is industrial-vintage, stay in that register
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter — banned generic defaults
- Don't add a stats bar ("50+ years | 1000+ items") — fabricated filler
- Don't use stock photos — real product images from blogTO and Curiocity are available
- Don't use a minimalist white layout — this store is PACKED with color and character
- Don't make it feel corporate or luxury — Courage My Love is unpretentious and accessible
- Don't bury the location info — Kensington Market is the #1 reason people visit

## Font Pairing
Heading: **Hammersmith One** — direct match from their Tumblr blog; bold, industrial-vintage sans-serif that conveys heritage and strength
Body: **Source Sans 3** — clean, highly readable sans-serif that balances Hammersmith's strong personality without competing
Nav: **Hammersmith One** at 400 weight, uppercase, 0.08em letter-spacing — matches the bold Tumblr nav style

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: White text on Courage Blue (#2A7CB8)
- Section alternation: Cream (#F5F0E6) → Sand (#E8DCC8) → White (#FFFFFF) → Dark footer (#2D1F14)
- Gallery section: Use dark espresso background (#2D1F14) to make colorful product photos pop
- Headings: Espresso (#2D1F14) on light backgrounds, cream (#F9ECBA) on dark backgrounds
- Accent highlights: Amber/gold (#D4A024) for star ratings, price callouts, special badges
- Never use: bright neon colors, pastels, corporate blue (#2563eb) — stay warm and earthy

## Layout Archetype: PORTFOLIO-FIRST
Rationale: The vintage treasures are the product. Visitors need to SEE the eclectic collection — beads, clothing, jewelry, antiques — to feel the pull to visit. The visual gallery drives foot traffic.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. **Hero** — Full-bleed storefront image with overlay text: "50 Years of Treasures" + Visit CTA
2. **Gallery** — Asymmetric grid of product photos on dark background — clothing, beads, china, jewelry
3. **Story** — Editorial split: large "Since 1975" typography left, family narrative right
4. **Products** — Category showcase: Vintage Clothing, Beads & Buttons, Jewelry, Antiques & Curiosities
5. **Team** — The Scriver family: Cece's photo, Stewart's story, three generations
6. **Reviews** — Customer testimonials on inverted dark section
7. **Visit** — Full map + hours + address, prominently featured
8. **Contact** — Phone, email, social links

## Layout Blueprint
- **Header**: Sticky, warm cream bg (#F5F0E6) with espresso text, Courage Blue logo/name left, nav right. On scroll, add subtle shadow.
- **Hero**: Full-bleed blue storefront photo with dark overlay gradient. Left-aligned: overline "EST. 1975 · KENSINGTON MARKET", large headline "Discover 50 Years of Treasures", CTA button "Visit Us"
- **Gallery**: Dark espresso background (#2D1F14). Asymmetric image grid — one large image + 4 smaller. Edge-to-edge photos. Minimal text captions.
- **Story**: Cream background. Editorial 2-column: Left has "SINCE 1975" in massive Hammersmith One. Right has the family story paragraphs.
- **Products**: White card grid on sand background. Each card: product photo top, category name, short description.
- **Team**: Sand background. Cece's real photo with bio. Stewart's initials on blue bg with his craftsmanship story.
- **Reviews**: Dark espresso background, cream text. Star ratings in amber. Verbatim customer quotes.
- **Visit**: Cream background. Split layout: Google Map embed left, hours/address/phone right.
- **Footer**: Espresso dark background. Contact info, social icons (Instagram SVG), Kensington Market location.

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "50 Years of Treasures from Around the World"
- Body copy should sound like: "Stewart still handcrafts one-of-a-kind jewelry at 86. That's the kind of care that goes into everything at Courage My Love."
- CTAs should sound like: "Come Explore" / "Visit the Shop" / "Discover the Collection"
