# TAYA Hair Salon — Design Directives

## Identity Summary
TAYA is a premium Korean hair salon in North York's Yonge & Sheppard corridor. Two locations, 15+ stylists, 1,300+ reviews averaging 4.6-4.9 stars. The brand is K-beauty luxury meets Toronto accessibility — warm, sophisticated, detail-oriented. The salon caters to a multicultural clientele with strong Korean roots. The aesthetic should feel like stepping into a high-end Seoul salon that speaks Toronto's language.

## Layout Personality: GALLERY-FORWARD + NEIGHBORHOOD Hybrid (B+D)
- Portfolio-driven: hair transformations are the product
- Warm, welcoming neighborhood feel — address/hours visible early
- Gallery-forward: work samples immediately visible, edge-to-edge
- Booking CTA above the fold

## DO
- Use warm gold (#C8A87C) as the signature accent throughout
- Use Cormorant Garamond for dramatic, elegant headings
- Use DM Sans for clean, readable body text
- Keep backgrounds warm (cream #FDFAF6, sand #F5EDE3) — never cold white
- Show transformation work prominently — gallery teaser on homepage
- Include both locations with separate hours
- Use dark espresso sections (#2A2118) for contrast (testimonials, footer)
- Make booking CTA prominent with gold gradient
- Use Korean/English bilingual touches (section labels)
- Reference K-beauty, Korean perm expertise in copy
- Use real Fresha images for gallery/atmosphere
- Keep service pricing transparent — it's a trust signal
- Use overline labels (uppercase, tracked) for section introductions

## DON'T
- Don't use cold whites or grays — this is a WARM brand
- Don't use dark mode as default — site is light-themed
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter
- Don't add stats bars ("10+ years", "500+ clients") — fabricated filler
- Don't use stock photos — Fresha images or CSS gradient fallbacks only
- Don't use equal-grid card layouts for gallery — asymmetric masonry
- Don't center everything — use left-aligned hero text for editorial feel
- Don't dump all sections on homepage — keep it tight (hero + teaser + CTA)
- Don't use anchor links — dedicated routes for every page
- Don't use rounded pill buttons — sharp 4px radius matches premium Korean aesthetic
- Don't use playful/casual fonts — the tone is sophisticated

## Font Pairing Rationale
- **Cormorant Garamond** (heading): Elegant, refined serif with beautiful hairlines. Reflects the precision and artistry of Korean hair styling. Not Playfair — Cormorant has more warmth and less overuse.
- **DM Sans** (body): Geometric sans-serif with subtle warmth. Clean enough for service info, friendly enough for the welcoming atmosphere. Not Inter — DM Sans has more character.

## Color Usage Rules
- **Gold (#C8A87C)**: Primary CTAs, accent borders, highlighted elements. Never as body text.
- **Espresso (#2A2118)**: Headings, dark section backgrounds, footer. High contrast partner to gold.
- **Warm cream (#FDFAF6)**: Main background. Never pure #FFFFFF for page bg.
- **Sand (#F5EDE3)**: Alternate section backgrounds for rhythm.
- **Muted brown (#8B7E72)**: Captions, timestamps, secondary info.

## Section Order (Homepage)
1. Hero — full-bleed salon image with dark overlay, left-aligned text, gold CTA
2. Gallery teaser — 3-4 transformation images linking to /gallery
3. Primary CTA — "Book Your Transformation" full-width gold section

## Tone of Voice Guide
- **Confident but warm**: "Your hair, perfected" not "We try our best"
- **K-beauty forward**: Reference Korean techniques naturally — K-perms, digital perms, Milbon treatments
- **Personal**: Highlight individual stylists by name — Lia, Anna, Jin, Ellie
- **Detail-focused**: Emphasize the consultation process, personalized approach
- **Bilingual touch**: Korean section labels add authenticity without alienating English speakers
