# Design Directives — The Toston Colombian Cuisine

## Identity Summary
The Toston is an intimate 18-seat Colombian restaurant in Markham run by husband-and-wife team Alexander Sandoval (chef) and Cristina Arbelaez (front of house). The brand is warm, authentic, and family-oriented — rooted in Colombian paisa food tradition and cafe culture. The design should feel like stepping into a cozy Colombian home, not a corporate chain restaurant. Warm earthy tones, real food photography, and a personal, inviting tone throughout.

## DO
- Use warm cream backgrounds (#FDF8F0) with deep coffee-brown text (#2C1810)
- Feature Colombian gold (#D4A843) for CTAs and accent highlights
- Show REAL food photography in every menu card — empanadas, bandeja paisa, ajiaco, tostones
- Lead with atmosphere — the cozy, intimate 18-seat space is a selling point
- Include hours and address prominently (location-first archetype)
- Use personal language — "Chef Alexander" and "Cristina" by name
- Keep sections warm and inviting with alternating cream tones
- Feature verbatim customer reviews that mention warmth and love
- Use the Colombian flag colors subtly (gold, blue, red) as accent system

## DON'T
- Don't use dark backgrounds — the brand is warm and light, not moody
- Don't use Playfair Display or Inter — those are generic defaults
- Don't use a stats bar — this is not a corporate site
- Don't use stock food photos — they have beautiful real food photography
- Don't use corporate language — "Schedule a consultation" or "Learn more" feel wrong
- Don't make the site feel like a chain restaurant — it's a family operation
- Don't bury the hours or address — foot traffic matters
- Don't use cold blues or greys — everything should feel warm (browns, golds, creams)
- Don't use sharp corners — moderate border-radius (8-12px) matches their welcoming feel

## Font Pairing
Heading: Cormorant Garamond — elegant serif that evokes traditional cafe culture and Colombian warmth
Body: Source Sans 3 — clean, readable sans-serif that pairs well with the serif headings, more personality than the Wix default Helvetica
Nav: Source Sans 3 at 600 weight uppercase — clear and structured navigation

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: Gold (#D4A843) on cream background — warm, inviting
- Section alternation: #FDF8F0 (cream) → #F5EDE0 (warm tan) → #FFFFFF (white cards)
- Footer: Deep coffee brown (#2C1810) with cream/gold text — cozy inversion
- Headings always in #2C1810 (coffee brown) on light backgrounds
- Never use cold grey (#808080) or pure black (#000000) for backgrounds
- Colombian red (#C0392B) for secondary accents and hover states only — don't overuse
- Colombian green (#2E7D32) sparingly for freshness indicators on menu items

## Layout Archetype: LOCATION-FIRST
Rationale: Colombian restaurant where foot traffic, neighborhood identity, and the intimate dining atmosphere are the primary draw. Customers describe it as a "hidden gem" and "hole in the wall" — discovery and location are key.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. **Hero** — Full-bleed food/atmosphere photo with overlay, restaurant name, tagline about authentic Colombian cuisine, dual CTA (View Menu + Visit Us)
2. **Menu Highlights** — 4-6 signature dishes with real food photos, prices, short descriptions
3. **About / Our Story** — Alexander & Cristina's story, cafe culture mission, the 18-seat intimate setting
4. **Reviews** — 4-6 verbatim Google reviews emphasizing warmth and authenticity
5. **Location + Hours** — Map embed, address, phone, hours table prominently displayed
6. **Contact** — Simple contact info, phone CTA

## Layout Blueprint
- Header: Sticky, solid cream background, logo/name left, nav right (Menu, About, Contact, Order), 72px height
- Hero: Full-bleed food atmosphere image with dark coffee-brown gradient overlay, centered white text, dual CTA buttons
- Menu section: 3-column grid of food cards with real photos, dish name, price, short description
- About section: Split layout — text left (their story), atmosphere photo right
- Reviews section: Card carousel/grid with customer quotes, star ratings, reviewer names
- Hours+Map section: Two-column — map embed left, hours table + address right
- Footer: Dark coffee-brown (#2C1810) with cream text, 3 columns (about, hours, contact)

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "Taste of Colombia in Markham" / "Made with Love, Served with Pride"
- Body copy should sound like: "Chef Alexander brings traditional paisa flavours to every plate, handmade fresh daily in our cozy kitchen."
- CTAs should sound like: "View Our Menu" / "Visit Us Today" / "Come Taste Colombia"
