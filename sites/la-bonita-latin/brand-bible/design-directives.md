# Design Directives — La Bonita Latin American Restaurant

## Identity Summary
La Bonita is a warm, family-owned Latin American restaurant in Mississauga, established in 2016. The owner followed her grandmother's footsteps, bringing authentic Colombian dishes to a cozy neighbourhood spot at Canadian Place. The brand radiates generosity, warmth, and Latin cultural pride — huge portions, fresh ingredients, and a family atmosphere where every guest feels at home. This is not fine dining; it's abuela's kitchen scaled up.

## DO
- Use warm, light backgrounds (#FFF9F2 off-white) — the Instagram grid shows bright food on clean surfaces
- Lead with vibrant food photography — the sancocho, churrasco platters, and bandeja paisa are the stars
- Use Latin red (#E8443A) for CTAs and accents — inspired by the South America silhouette in their logo
- Use gold/yellow (#F7B731) as secondary warmth — Colombian flag reference, tropical sunshine
- Keep copy warm and family-oriented — "grandmother's recipes", "made with love", "cozy atmosphere"
- Show hours and address prominently — cash/e-transfer only is important info
- Include bilingual touches where natural (Spanish dish names, "Bienvenidos")
- Highlight the generous portions — every review mentions this
- Feature the tres leches cake — it's their most-praised dessert

## DON'T
- Don't use dark backgrounds as the dominant theme — the brand is warm and inviting, not moody
- Don't use corporate or fine-dining language — this is a cozy family spot, not upscale
- Don't use Playfair Display or Inter — these are generic defaults
- Don't include fake statistics or star counts — only use real data (4.3/5 Google, 851 reviews)
- Don't show empty placeholder images — every card must have a real food photo
- Don't use sharp, angular design — rounded corners (8-12px) match the friendly warmth
- Don't bury the cash/e-transfer payment info — it's a common surprise for first-time visitors

## Font Pairing
Heading: Libre Baskerville — because the storefront sign uses a serif/script "La" with classic letterforms; Libre Baskerville provides established warmth appropriate for a family restaurant since 2016
Body: Nunito Sans — because the "Bonita" text on the storefront uses a clean sans-serif; Nunito Sans is rounded and friendly, matching the warm, approachable personality
Nav: Nunito Sans at 600 weight, uppercase — clean and readable navigation

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: #E8443A (Latin red) on #FFF9F2 (off-white) or white
- Section alternation: #FFF9F2 (warm off-white) -> #FDF0E1 (warmer cream) -> #FFF9F2
- Footer: #2B1810 (espresso) background with #FFF9F2 text and #F7B731 gold accents
- Gold accents: #F7B731 for prices, highlights, and secondary CTAs
- Green accent: #2D8E4E sparingly for freshness indicators or Colombian flag references
- Never use bright blue or purple — these clash with the warm Latin palette

## Layout Archetype: LOCATION-FIRST
Rationale: La Bonita is a neighbourhood restaurant where foot traffic, hours of operation, and the dining atmosphere drive visits. Customers describe it as a "hidden gem" — discovery and location are key.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. Hero — full-bleed food photography with warm overlay, restaurant name, and tagline
2. Menu Highlights — 4 signature dishes with real photos, names, prices, descriptions
3. About/Story — grandmother's legacy, established 2016, owner's passion
4. Gallery — food and storefront photos showing the real La Bonita
5. Reviews — verbatim customer quotes highlighting portions, authenticity, and warmth
6. Map + Hours — Google Maps embed, full weekly hours, cash/e-transfer note
7. Contact — phone, address, Instagram link

## Layout Blueprint
- Header: Sticky, warm off-white (#FFF9F2) with backdrop blur, logo left, nav right, "Call Us" CTA button
- Hero: Full-bleed food photo (churrasco platter), dark warm overlay, centered text with dual CTA buttons
- Content sections: Alternating #FFF9F2 and #FDF0E1 backgrounds, 80px vertical padding, 1200px max-width
- Footer: Dark espresso (#2B1810), 3-column layout (about, hours, contact), gold accents

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "A Taste of Latin America in Mississauga"
- Body copy should sound like: "Every dish is made fresh daily, following the same recipes my grandmother perfected in her own restaurant."
- CTAs should sound like: "Explore Our Menu" / "Come Visit Us" / "Call to Order"
