# EFX Salon and Spa — Design Directives

## Identity Summary
EFX Salon and Spa is an established, full-service beauty salon in Etobicoke's Wincott Drive community. Owned by Lisa (36+ years experience), the salon projects warm professionalism, European-inspired elegance, and inclusive community spirit. They are a Kerastase Partner and champion clean, sustainable products. The vibe is premium but approachable — not luxury-exclusive, but elevated neighborhood salon.

## DO
- Use a light, airy background (white/off-white) — the real site is light-themed
- Feature deep purple (#4d004d) as the primary accent — it is their CSS primary color
- Use rose mauve (#C48981) as a warm secondary accent for hover states and soft touches
- Add warm gold (#D4A76A) for CTAs and premium highlights
- Use Poppins for headings (found on their real site as subtitle font, promoted for hierarchy)
- Use Abel for body text (their actual body font)
- Show team expertise prominently — 5 named stylists with real experience
- Emphasize "Where Everyone Is Welcome" — their inclusive positioning
- Feature product lines (Kerastase, Eufora) as trust signals
- Use warm, textured section backgrounds (#FBF8F5) for an elegant European feel
- Include booking CTA above the fold
- Show hours and location prominently (neighborhood salon)
- Use uppercase nav with letter-spacing (matches their nav style)
- Keep border-radius moderate (4px buttons, 8px cards) — not too rounded, not sharp

## DON'T
- Don't use dark mode — the real site is light/white themed
- Don't use Playfair Display, Roboto, or Inter — not on their real site
- Don't use stock photos from Unsplash — use their real images
- Don't add a stats/metrics bar — not on their real site
- Don't use overly playful or casual tone — they project professionalism
- Don't use sharp/edgy design elements — the brand is warm and elegant
- Don't add "As Seen In" press logos — not applicable
- Don't create fake testimonials — use only verified review snippets
- Don't use very rounded buttons (pill shape) — keep it refined at 4px
- Don't use anchor navigation — every page gets its own route

## Font Pairing
- **Headings**: Poppins (600-700) — clean geometric sans-serif with personality, found on their real site
- **Body**: Abel (400) — their actual body font, legible and warm
- **Nav**: Poppins (600, uppercase, tracking 0.08em) — professional navigation feel
- **Rationale**: Both fonts were found on the real EFX website. Poppins brings structure and modernity to headings while Abel provides a lighter, approachable body text. The pairing feels elegant without being stuffy.

## Color Usage Rules
- **Deep Purple (#4d004d)**: Hero overlays, footer, section accent backgrounds, CTA borders
- **Rose Mauve (#C48981)**: Hover states, decorative accents, secondary CTAs, dividers
- **Warm Gold (#D4A76A)**: Primary CTA buttons, highlights, premium badges
- **White (#FFFFFF)**: Main page background, card backgrounds
- **Off-white (#FBF8F5)**: Warm alternating section backgrounds
- **Dark text (#333333)**: Headings on light backgrounds
- **Body text (#555555)**: Paragraph text
- **White text (#FFFFFF)**: Text on purple/dark sections

## Layout Archetype: GALLERY-FORWARD + NEIGHBORHOOD
A hybrid approach: Gallery-forward because the salon's work (styling, coloring, transformations) is the product and visual proof drives bookings. Neighborhood because this is a community salon on Wincott Drive where hours, location, and approachability matter.

## Section Order
1. Hero (full-bleed with purple overlay, booking CTA)
2. Services teaser (3-4 key services, link to /services)
3. Gallery teaser (3-4 transformation images, link to /gallery)
4. Team preview (owner + 1-2 senior stylists, link to /team)
5. Primary CTA (book appointment)

## Layout Blueprint
- **Header**: Sticky white, logo left, nav right, deep purple accents
- **Hero**: Full-bleed hair transformation image with purple gradient overlay, left-aligned text
- **Services**: Editorial list with dividers — NOT a 4-card grid
- **Gallery**: Dark/deep purple background section to make hair photos pop
- **Team**: Warm off-white background, initials-on-purple for missing photos
- **Contact**: Map as featured element, hours prominent, booking CTA
- **Footer**: Deep purple background, three columns, social SVG icons

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Professional but warm — like a trusted stylist talking to a loyal client
- Inclusive: "Where Everyone Is Welcome" is their actual positioning
- Confident without being boastful: "36 years of excellence" not "the best salon ever"
- Community-rooted: reference Etobicoke/Wincott Dr naturally
- Product-educated: mention Kerastase, Eufora as quality signals
- Avoid: discount language, urgency/pressure tactics, overly corporate speak
