# Design Directives — Neelkanth Beauty Parlour

## Identity Summary
Neelkanth Beauty Parlour is a warm, welcoming South Asian beauty salon in Markham's New Delhi Dr corridor. Their brand is feminine, nurturing, and professional — red and white with elegant script typography. The logo features a flowing red script "Neelkanth" with a woman silhouette. The vibe is affordable luxury with genuine warmth — they offer tea and snacks during appointments. This is NOT a high-fashion salon; it's a neighborhood gem where clients feel pampered and cared for like family.

## DO
- Use warm, light backgrounds throughout (white + warm cream #FFF8F0)
- Keep the red (#B00910) as the primary accent — it's their brand identity
- Use the hero stock image they already have (beauty portrait) with proper dark overlay for text readability
- Include ALL services with prices — this is a price-driven business
- Emphasize the warm hospitality angle (complimentary tea, personalized service)
- Use the real logo PNG from their CDN
- Include WhatsApp booking as a CTA option (they use it actively)
- Reference the South Asian community context — "New Delhi Dr" neighborhood
- Use soft, warm section transitions with cream backgrounds
- Include bridal makeup as a featured service — it's a differentiator

## DON'T
- Don't use dark mode — their site is clean white/light
- Don't use angular/brutalist design — their brand is soft and feminine
- Don't use Roboto or Inter — stick to their actual fonts (Playfair Display, Source Sans Pro, Montserrat)
- Don't fabricate team members — no staff names were found
- Don't add stats bars — no verified stats to show
- Don't make it look clinical/medical — it's a warm beauty parlour
- Don't use cold blue or green tones — warm red and gold only
- Don't skip the service prices — they're a key selling point
- Don't use Unsplash images — use their actual hero image and logo

## Font Pairing
Heading: Playfair Display — because they use it on their actual site, elegant serif that matches the beauty/feminine aesthetic
Body: Source Sans Pro — because they use it on their actual site, clean and readable sans-serif
Nav: Montserrat 600 weight, uppercase — because they use it for UI elements, modern and structured

## Color Usage Rules
- Primary CTA: #B00910 red on white background — high contrast, matches logo
- Section alternation: #FFFFFF (white) → #FFF8F0 (warm cream) → #FFFFFF → #F6F6F6 (light gray)
- Footer: #1B1B1B dark background with white text — visual anchor
- Gold accent #C9A84C: Use ONLY for bridal section highlights and premium touches — never overuse
- Never use blue for CTA — red is their brand color
- Hero text: white on dark overlay over the beauty portrait image

## Layout Archetype: PORTFOLIO-FIRST + NEIGHBORHOOD hybrid
Rationale: Beauty parlour where work quality is the product, but foot traffic and neighborhood identity also matter. Leads with visual appeal, includes location/hours prominently.

## Section Order (from archetype)
1. Hero — Full-bleed beauty image with dark overlay, tagline "Your Beauty, Our Passion", Book Now CTA
2. Services Teaser — Top 3-4 service categories with starting prices, link to /services
3. Gallery Teaser — 3-4 beauty images in asymmetric grid, link to /gallery
4. Reviews — Customer praise about warmth and skill
5. CTA — Full-bleed red section with booking call-to-action
6. Contact preview — Hours, address, WhatsApp

## Layout Blueprint
- Header: White background with red logo (logo has red script + black text on white bg — needs WHITE header), sticky, Montserrat nav links
- Hero: Full-bleed beauty portrait with 55% dark overlay, centered white text, large Playfair Display heading
- Services: Warm cream background, editorial-style service list (not card grid)
- Gallery: Dark background section to make beauty images pop
- Testimonials: Alternating background, large quote typography
- CTA: Full-bleed red (#B00910) background with white text
- Footer: Dark #1B1B1B with 3 columns — contact, hours, social

## Tone of Voice Guide
- Headlines should sound like: "Your Beauty, Our Passion" — warm, aspirational, personal
- Body copy should sound like: "Step into our cozy parlour and let our skilled team pamper you with expert threading, facials, and bridal makeup. Complimentary chai while you wait."
- CTAs should sound like: "Book Your Glow-Up" / "Call for an Appointment" / "WhatsApp Us"
